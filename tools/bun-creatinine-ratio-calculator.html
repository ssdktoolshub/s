📚 Clinical Pathology Notes: BUN/Creatinine Ratio

What is the clinical value of the BUN/Creatinine ratio?
This ratio helps distinguish the cause of kidney injury. BUN and creatinine are both filtered by the glomerulus, but BUN is reabsorbed in tubules while creatinine is not. Under dehydrating (pre-renal) conditions, tubules absorb extra water and urea, causing the BUN to rise faster than creatinine, thus widening the ratio.

How are the ranges classified?
- **Normal**: 10:1 to 20:1
- **High Ratio (>20:1)**: Suggests pre-renal causes (dehydration, congestive heart failure, high protein breakdown).
- **Low Ratio (<10:1)**: Suggests intrinsic renal injury (acute tubular necrosis) or liver disease.
