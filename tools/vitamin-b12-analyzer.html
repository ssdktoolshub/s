📚 Clinical Pathology Notes: Vitamin B12

Why is Vitamin B12 critical?
Vitamin B12 is essential for DNA synthesis, red blood cell production, and neurological development. It maintains the myelin sheath that covers nerves, and deficiency can lead to irreversible nerve damage if untreated.

What is Pernicious Anemia?
Pernicious anemia is an autoimmune condition where the stomach's parietal cells or intrinsic factor (essential for B12 absorption in the small intestine) are destroyed by antibodies. It leads to severe B12 deficiency despite high dietary intake.

What dietary factors affect B12?
Vitamin B12 is primarily found in animal products (meat, fish, eggs, dairy). Strict vegetarians and vegans are at high risk of dietary deficiency and should consume fortified foods or daily supplements. Metformin and acid-blockers (PPIs) also reduce absorption.
