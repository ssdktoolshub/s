📚 Clinical Pathology Notes: Potassium

Why is Potassium critical?
Potassium is the chief intracellular electrolyte, responsible for cardiac electrophysiology, muscle contractions, and nerve conduction. Very small changes in blood potassium can affect heart rhythm.

What are the clinical limits?
- **Hypokalemia (< 3.5 mEq/L)**: Low potassium, commonly caused by water loss (diarrhea/vomiting) or loop diuretic medicines. It can cause muscle weakness or arrhythmia.
- **Hyperkalemia (> 5.1 mEq/L)**: High potassium, often seen in kidney failure (inability to excrete potassium) or tissue trauma. It poses severe cardiac risks.
