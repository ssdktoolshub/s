📚 Clinical Pathology Notes: Alanine Aminotransferase (ALT)

What is ALT/SGPT?
ALT (Alanine Aminotransferase), historically called SGPT, is an enzyme concentrated mostly in liver cells. When liver cells are damaged, they release ALT into the bloodstream, elevating serum levels.

What are the reference limits?
Normal ranges typically fall under 40 U/L for adult males and under 35 U/L for adult females. Values can vary slightly based on laboratory instrumentation.
