📚 Clinical Pathology Notes: Amylase

What is Serum Amylase?
Amylase is a digestive enzyme that catalyses the hydrolysis of starch into sugars. It is produced predominantly by the acinar cells of the pancreas and by the salivary glands. A small quantity is cleared continuously by the kidneys.

When does Amylase elevate?
Serum amylase rises rapidly (within 2 to 12 hours) following pancreatic cell injury or duct blockage, peaking at 12 to 72 hours. Elevations above 3 times the upper limit of normal (~300 U/L) are highly characteristic of acute pancreatitis. Other causes include salivary duct stones, mumps, or gallstone complications.

What does low Amylase mean?
Pathologically low amylase values (< 28 U/L) are less common but can point to chronic, permanent damage to pancreatic tissue (such as end-stage chronic pancreatitis, cystic fibrosis) or severe liver disease.
