📚 Clinical Pathology Notes: Urobilinogen

What is Urobilinogen?
Urobilinogen is a colorless byproduct of bilirubin reduction. It is formed in the intestines by bacterial action on bilirubin. Most of it is excreted in feces, but a small amount is reabsorbed and excreted in urine.

What is the clinical significance of Urine Urobilinogen?
Elevated urine urobilinogen indicates excess bilirubin production (like hemolysis) or hepatic inability to clear normal reabsorbed urobilinogen. An absent level indicates that no bilirubin is reaching the gut, usually signifying a complete biliary tract blockage.
