📚 Clinical Pathology Notes: HbA1c to eAG

What is eAG?
eAG (Estimated Average Glucose) represents your average blood glucose levels over the past 2 to 3 months, calculated directly from your HbA1c percentage. It helps patients relate their HbA1c readings to their daily fingerstick or continuous glucose monitor (CGM) readings.

What is the ADAG formula used for this?
The American Diabetes Association (ADA) and other global bodies utilize the ADAG equation:
eAG (mg/dL) = (28.7 × HbA1c) - 46.7.
