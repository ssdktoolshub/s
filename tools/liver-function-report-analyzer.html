📚 Clinical Pathology Notes: Liver Panels

How do ALT/AST and ALP differentiate liver disorders?
- **Hepatocellular Pattern**: Marked elevations in ALT and AST (transaminases) relative to ALP suggests liver cell inflammation/injury (hepatitis, toxins).
- **Cholestatic Pattern**: Marked elevations in Alkaline Phosphatase (ALP) and direct bilirubin relative to ALT/AST suggest bile duct obstruction or sludge.

What determines hepatic synthetic capacity?
Because albumin and clotting factors are synthesized solely by hepatocytes, serum Albumin and Total Protein levels are key indicators of the liver's long-term production capability. Chronic liver failure (cirrhosis) typically presents with hypoalbuminemia.
