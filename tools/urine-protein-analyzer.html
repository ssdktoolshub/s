📚 Clinical Pathology Notes: Proteinuria

What is Proteinuria?
Proteinuria is the presence of abnormal quantities of proteins (primarily albumin) in the urine. Healthy glomeruli restrict large proteins from passing into urine. Spillage indicates kidney filtration filter strain.

What are the clinical classifications?
- **Microalbuminuria (30 - 300 mg/24h)**: Early kidney damage indicator, crucial to monitor in diabetes or hypertension.
- **Macroalbuminuria (> 300 mg/24h)**: Established proteinuria.
- **Nephrotic Range (> 3500 mg/24h)**: Severe protein loss causing body edema (swelling) and low blood albumin, requiring urgent care.
