📚 Clinical Pathology Notes: TSH

What is TSH?
TSH (Thyroid Stimulating Hormone) is produced by the pituitary gland and regulates the thyroid gland. It instructs the thyroid to produce T3 and T4 hormones. TSH is the most sensitive first-line test for thyroid dysfunction.

How does TSH reflect thyroid function?
High TSH indicates the pituitary is working harder because the thyroid is underactive (Hypothyroidism). Low TSH indicates the thyroid is overactive, suppressing pituitary output (Hyperthyroidism). TSH operates via a negative feedback loop with T3/T4.

What is Subclinical vs Overt Hypothyroidism?
Subclinical Hypothyroidism: TSH 4.0–10.0 with normal fT4 — mild dysfunction, may not need treatment. Overt Hypothyroidism: TSH > 10.0 with low fT4 — significant thyroid failure requiring thyroid hormone replacement therapy.
