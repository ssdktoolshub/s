📚 Clinical Pathology Notes: Kidney Panels

Why analyze KFT parameters collectively?
An isolated high creatinine or urea might simply represent temporary dehydration. Looking at eGFR, BUN/Creatinine Ratio, and Serum Electrolytes together helps determine whether the cause is pre-renal (dehydration/heart strain) or renal (intrinsic kidney damage).

What role do Sodium and Potassium play?
Kidneys regulate electrolyte balance. Chronic kidney diseases impair potassium excretion, leading to high blood potassium (hyperkalemia), which can affect heart rhythm and requires monitoring.
