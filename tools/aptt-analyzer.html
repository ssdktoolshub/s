📚 Clinical Pathology Notes: aPTT

What is aPTT?
Activated Partial Thromboplastin Time (aPTT) evaluates the intrinsic and common pathways of blood coagulation. It measures the seconds required for fibrin clot formation after the activation of contact factors in plasma.

What is the significance of prolonged aPTT?
Prolonged aPTT indicates deficiencies in specific clotting factors (VIII, IX, XI, XII), presence of an inhibitor (like lupus anticoagulant), or heparin blood-thinning therapy. Critical values (>80 seconds) carry serious immediate bleeding hazards.
