📚 Clinical Pathology Notes: Widal Serology

What is the Widal Test?
The Widal test is a serological method used to diagnose typhoid fever by measuring agglutinating antibodies against Salmonella enterica serotype Typhi (Somatic 'O' and Flagellar 'H' antigens).

Understanding Somatic 'O' and Flagellar 'H' Antigens
The somatic 'O' antigen is a cell wall component that rises during acute stages. The flagellar 'H' antigen represents flagellar proteins and stays elevated for longer periods, often indicating late infection stages, past infection, or immunization.

How to Interpret Agglutination Titers?
In most endemic regions, a titer of 1:80 is considered borderline or a baseline exposure, while titers of 1:160 or higher for either 'O' or 'H' are clinically significant and highly suggestive of active enteric fever. Single tests may be inconclusive; paired testing 7-10 days apart is preferred.
