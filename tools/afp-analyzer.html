📚 Clinical Pathology Notes: AFP

What is Alpha-Fetoprotein (AFP)?
AFP is a major plasma protein produced by the yolk sac and liver of a developing fetus. It is the fetal analog of serum albumin. In healthy non-pregnant adults, AFP levels are extremely low. Elevated levels serve as a primary tumor marker.

When is AFP clinically elevated?
AFP rises significantly in Hepatocellular Carcinoma (primary liver cancer) and non-seminomatous germ cell tumors (testicular or ovarian origin). It is also temporarily elevated during pregnancy, liver regeneration (e.g. recovering from acute hepatitis), and in chronic liver disease.
