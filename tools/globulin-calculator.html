📚 Clinical Pathology Notes: Serum Globulins

What are Globulins?
Globulins are a diverse group of proteins in blood plasma. They are divided into alpha-1, alpha-2, beta, and gamma globulins. The gamma globulins represent antibodies produced by immune system plasma cells.

How is Globulin calculated?
Globulin is not measured directly in standard panels. Instead, it is calculated by subtracting measured Albumin from Total Protein:
Globulin = Total Protein - Albumin.
