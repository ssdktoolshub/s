📚 Clinical Pathology Notes: Calcium

What is Calcium's role?
Calcium is critical for bone mineralization, neuromuscular transmission, cellular signaling, and blood coagulation. It is regulated tightly by parathyroid hormone (PTH) and Vitamin D.

What are Hypocalcemia and Hypercalcemia?
- **Hypocalcemia (< 8.5 mg/dL)**: Low calcium, often caused by Vitamin D deficiency, hypoparathyroidism, or low albumin. It can cause muscle tetany and tingling.
- **Hypercalcemia (> 10.2 mg/dL)**: High calcium, commonly caused by hyperparathyroidism or malignancy. It can cause kidney stones and heart conduction anomalies.
