📚 Clinical Pathology Notes: Uric Acid

What is Uric Acid?
Uric acid is a waste product formed from the metabolic breakdown of purines, which are natural chemical compounds found in cells and certain foods (like red meat, seafood, and alcohol).

What is Hyperuricemia and Gout?
Hyperuricemia occurs when there is too much uric acid in the blood. If levels remain high, uric acid can form needle-like crystals in joints, causing a painful inflammatory arthritis known as gout, or in kidneys, leading to kidney stones.
