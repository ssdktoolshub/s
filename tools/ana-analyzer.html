📚 Clinical Pathology Notes: Antinuclear Antibodies

What is ANA?
Antinuclear Antibodies (ANA) are autoantibodies that target components inside a cell's nucleus. An ANA screening test is the standard entry-level tool when investigating systemic autoimmune conditions.

Understanding Titers (e.g. 1:40 vs. 1:1280)
The titer represents how many times the patient's blood serum had to be diluted with saline to no longer see the antibodies under a microscope. A dilution of 1:1280 contains many more autoantibodies than 1:40. Higher titers are far more strongly correlated with actual autoimmune disease.

Benign Positives and False Positives
Low titers (1:40 and 1:80) are frequently benign. Around 20-30% of the healthy population (especially women and the elderly) has a positive low-titer ANA without any underlying illness. Certain medications, thyroid problems, and viral recovery states also trigger positive results.
