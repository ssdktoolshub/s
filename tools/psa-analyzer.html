📚 Clinical Pathology Notes: PSA

What is PSA?
Prostate-Specific Antigen (PSA) is a glycoprotein enzyme secreted by the epithelial cells of the prostate gland. It liquefies semen in the seminal coagulum and is present in small quantities in the serum of healthy men.

Why does PSA increase with age?
Prostate volume increases as men age (Benign Prostatic Hyperplasia), which naturally leads to higher PSA synthesis and leakage into the blood stream. Age-specific reference ranges prevent overdiagnosis and unnecessary biopsies in older men.
