📚 Clinical Pathology Notes: Albumin/Globulin Ratio

What is the A/G Ratio?
It is the ratio of Albumin concentration to Globulin concentration in blood. Because total protein is composed mainly of these two proteins, this ratio is useful in identifying shifts in protein synthesis.

How are low and high values interpreted?
- **Low A/G Ratio (< 1.1)**: Occurs if Albumin drops (cirrhosis, kidney disease) or Globulin increases (multiple myeloma, chronic inflammation, autoimmune reactions).
- **High A/G Ratio (> 2.5)**: Occurs with low globulin levels (immunoglobulin deficiencies) or severe dehydration.
