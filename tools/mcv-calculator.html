📚 Clinical Pathology Notes: Mean Corpuscular Volume (MCV)

What is MCV?
Mean Corpuscular Volume (MCV) is the average size of your red blood cells. It is calculated by dividing your Hematocrit percentage by your total RBC count. It is a fundamental index used to classify anemias.

How is it calculated mathematically?
The formula is: MCV (fL) = (Hematocrit (%) × 10) / RBC (Million/µL). For instance, if Hct is 45% and RBC count is 5.0, then MCV is `(45 × 10) / 5 = 90 fL`.

What are the three cellular classifications?
1. **Microcytic (MCV < 80 fL)**: Red cells are abnormally small (commonly iron deficiency or thalassemia).
2. **Normocytic (MCV 80 - 100 fL)**: Cells are of normal size (acute bleeding, chronic disease anemia).
3. **Macrocytic (MCV > 100 fL)**: Cells are abnormally large (B12 or folate deficiency).
