📚 Clinical Pathology Notes: Ferritin

What is Ferritin?
Ferritin is a blood protein that stores iron inside body cells. Measuring serum ferritin is the most accurate indirect reflection of total body iron stores. Low levels signify iron deficiency before manifest anemia occurs.

What is Ferritin Overload?
Excessive ferritin levels can indicate hereditary hemochromatosis (iron overload disorder) or, more commonly, systemic inflammation, acute infection, or active liver disease since ferritin acts as an acute-phase reactant.
