📚 Clinical Pathology Notes: Hemoglobin (Hb)

What is Hemoglobin?
Hemoglobin is the iron-rich protein in red blood cells that binds oxygen in the lungs and carries it to tissues and organs throughout the body. It also returns carbon dioxide from tissues back to the lungs.

How is it measured?
It is measured in grams per deciliter (g/dL). Low levels indicate that your blood has lower oxygen-carrying capacity (anemia), while high levels indicate erythrocytosis or dehydration.

What affects Hemoglobin levels?
Nutritional intake of iron and vitamins, kidney health (which produces erythropoietin), hydration status, chronic pulmonary conditions, smoking, and blood loss due to various factors.
