📚 Clinical Pathology Notes: Estradiol

What is Estradiol (E2)?
Estradiol is the most active and dominant form of estrogen in adult, non-pregnant females. It is primarily synthesized by the granulosa cells of the ovarian follicles. Small amounts are generated in males via peripheral aromatization of testosterone.

How does it fluctuate?
In menstruating females, E2 peaks twice: once in the late follicular phase (triggering LH surge and ovulation) and a secondary, broader peak during the luteal phase. After menopause, ovarian production ceases, resulting in persistently low levels.

What causes abnormal levels?
High estradiol levels may indicate estrogen therapy, ovarian cysts, or tumors. In men, elevated estradiol causes gynecomastia and is linked to liver cirrhosis. Low estradiol is a hallmark of ovarian failure, menopause, extreme eating disorders, or heavy athletic training.
