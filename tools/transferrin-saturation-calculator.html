📚 Clinical Pathology Notes: Transferrin Saturation

What is Transferrin Saturation (TSAT)?
Transferrin Saturation (TSAT) measures the percentage of transferrin's binding sites that are occupied by iron. It indicates how much iron is readily available to be transported to tissues and bone marrow for red blood cell production.

Why calculate TSAT instead of using Serum Iron alone?
Serum iron alone is highly variable throughout the day and is affected by diet. TSAT evaluates iron levels relative to the carrier capacity (TIBC), providing a more reliable clinical picture of iron deficiency or iron overload than serum iron alone.
