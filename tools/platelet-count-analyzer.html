📚 Clinical Pathology Notes: Platelets (Thrombocytes)

What are Platelets?
Platelets (or thrombocytes) are tiny, disc-shaped cell fragments produced in the bone marrow. Their primary function is to clump together and form plugs (clots) to seal broken blood vessels and stop bleeding.

What is Thrombocytopenia?
Thrombocytopenia refers to a abnormally low platelet count. Without enough platelets, blood cannot clot properly, resulting in high risks of bruising, skin spots (petechiae), or spontaneous nose/gum bleeding.

What is Thrombocytosis?
Thrombocytosis is an elevated platelet count. It can be reactive (due to temporary infection or iron deficiency) or primary (due to bone marrow clonal disorders). It increases risks of thrombus (blood clots forming inside veins or arteries).
