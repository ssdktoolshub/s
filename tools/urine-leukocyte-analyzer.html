📚 Clinical Notes: Leukocyte Esterase in Urine

What is Leukocyte Esterase?
Leukocyte esterase is an enzyme released by white blood cells (neutrophils). Its presence in urine — detected by the dipstick — indicates pyuria (WBCs in urine), which is a hallmark of urinary tract inflammation or infection.

What is pyuria?
Pyuria is defined as 5 or more WBCs per high-power field (HPF) on urine microscopy, or a positive leukocyte esterase on dipstick. It indicates active inflammation in the urinary tract and warrants further investigation.

How does combined testing with nitrite improve accuracy?
Leukocyte esterase positive + nitrite positive has a specificity of over 90% for bacterial UTI. Leukocyte esterase alone can be positive in non-bacterial causes (interstitial nephritis, urethritis, kidney stones) — making combined testing essential for accurate triage.
