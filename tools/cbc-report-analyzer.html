📚 Clinical Pathology Notes: Understanding CBC

What is a Complete Blood Count (CBC) test?
A CBC is a comprehensive blood test measuring three primary cells: Red Blood Cells (carrying oxygen), White Blood Cells (fighting infection), and Platelets (forming clots). It provides key details on hematological health and is crucial in detecting nutritional deficiencies, toxicities, anemias, and immunological responses.

Why do doctors order a CBC?
It is requested during routine wellness checkups, prior to surgical operations, or when evaluating clinical symptoms like unexplained weakness, persistent fatigue, fever, bruising, inflammation, or bone marrow distress.

What are RBC Indices (MCV, MCH, MCHC)?
These are calculated ratios reflecting size and color properties of red blood cells. MCV (Mean Cell Volume) represents the size of RBCs, MCH (Mean Cell Hemoglobin) indicates the mass of hemoglobin per cell, and MCHC (Mean Cell Hemoglobin Concentration) reflects cellular hemoglobin concentration. Together with RDW (Red Cell Distribution Width), they differentiate microcytic, macrocytic, and normocytic anemias.
