📚 Clinical Pathology Notes: Lipids

What is the significance of a full lipid panel?
A full fasting lipid panel evaluates all primary cholesterol fractions (LDL, HDL, VLDL) and triglycerides. Analyzing these values in unison allows calculating specific risk ratios (Cholesterol/HDL, TG/HDL) which correlate strongly with cardiovascular outcomes.

What are the primary lipid patterns?
- **Hypercholesterolemia**: Isolated high Total Cholesterol or LDL.
- **Hypertriglyceridemia**: Isolated high Triglycerides, often carbohydrate-driven.
- **Mixed Hyperlipidemia**: Elevated levels of both cholesterol and triglycerides, carrying high vascular plaque risk.
