📚 Clinical Pathology Notes: Albumin

What is Albumin?
Albumin is the most abundant protein in human blood plasma. It is produced solely by liver cells and performs key physiological roles, including maintaining oncotic pressure (keeping fluids inside blood vessels) and transporting hormones, drugs, and calcium.

What is Hypoalbuminemia?
Low albumin (hypoalbuminemia) commonly indicates hepatic synthetic dysfunction (e.g. liver cirrhosis), renal loss (nephrotic syndrome), chronic systemic inflammation, or severe dietary protein deficiency.
