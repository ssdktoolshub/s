📚 Clinical Pathology Notes: Total Cholesterol

What is Total Cholesterol?
Total Cholesterol is the sum of all cholesterol in your blood, including LDL (bad), HDL (good), and VLDL. It is used as a screening tool to assess cardiovascular plaque risks.

What are the reference limits?
- **Desirable (< 200 mg/dL)**: Associated with lower cardiovascular plaque risks.
- **Borderline High (200 - 239 mg/dL)**: Indicates moderate lifestyle adjustments are needed.
- **High (≥ 240 mg/dL)**: Elevated risk of atherosclerosis. Requires clinical review.
