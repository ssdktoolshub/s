📚 Clinical Pathology Notes: Free T3

What is Free T3 (fT3)?
Free T3 (Triiodothyronine) is the active form of the T3 thyroid hormone circulating in the blood. Although it makes up only a small fraction of total T3, it is responsible for regulating metabolism, body temperature, and heart rate.

When is Free T3 tested?
Free T3 is usually measured to evaluate hyperthyroidism (overactive thyroid) or to monitor patients receiving thyroid hormone therapy. It is particularly helpful in diagnosing T3 Thyrotoxicosis (where TSH is low, fT4 is normal, but fT3 is high).

What is the relationship between T3, T4, and TSH?
TSH from the pituitary gland stimulates the thyroid to release T4 (mostly inactive reservoir) and T3 (active hormone). Most T3 is converted from T4 in peripheral tissues. A negative feedback loop ensures that high T3/T4 levels lower TSH, while low T3/T4 levels raise TSH.
