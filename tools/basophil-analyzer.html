📚 Clinical Pathology Notes: Basophils

What are Basophils?
Basophils are the rarest type of granulocytic white blood cell in circulation. They contain large dark-blue granules packed with histamine (triggers vasodilation in allergies) and heparin (prevents rapid clotting).

What is Basophilia?
Basophilia is an elevated basophil count (relative > 1% or absolute > 100 cells/µL). It is rare and points to severe allergic diseases, chronic inflammation, or bone marrow disorders like chronic myeloid leukemia.

Does Basopenia matter?
Basopenia is a low count (< 0.5% relative). Because healthy individuals frequently have basophil percentages close to 0%, basopenia is rarely of clinical concern unless associated with severe shock or steroid excess.
