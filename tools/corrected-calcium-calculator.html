📚 Clinical Pathology Notes: Albumin-Corrected Calcium

Why correct calcium for low albumin?
Approximately 40-50% of serum calcium is bound to blood proteins, primarily Albumin. When albumin levels are abnormally low (hypoalbuminemia), total measured calcium appears falsely low, despite the active 'ionized' calcium remaining normal. Correcting the calcium levels helps prevent misdiagnosing hypocalcemia.

What is the correction equation?
The standard correction equation is:
Corrected Calcium (mg/dL) = Measured Calcium (mg/dL) + 0.8 × (4.0 - Albumin in g/dL) (Only applied if Albumin is less than 4.0 g/dL).
