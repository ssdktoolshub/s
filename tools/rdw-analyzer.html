📚 Clinical Pathology Notes: Red Cell Distribution Width (RDW)

What is RDW?
RDW (Red Cell Distribution Width) measures the variation in the size of your red blood cells. Normally, red blood cells are all roughly the same size. A high RDW means there is a large difference between the smallest and largest cells, a condition called anisocytosis.

What is Anisocytosis?
Anisocytosis is the medical term for having red blood cells of unequal sizes. High RDW is the quantitative measure of anisocytosis, which frequently signals active nutritional anemias as new, malformed cells enter circulation.

How is RDW used alongside MCV?
Combining RDW and MCV is extremely powerful:
- **High RDW + Low MCV**: Highly suggestive of iron deficiency anemia.
- **Normal RDW + Low MCV**: Suggestive of genetic Thalassemia trait (where cells are uniformly small).
- **High RDW + High MCV**: Suggests Vitamin B12 or Folate deficiency anemia.
