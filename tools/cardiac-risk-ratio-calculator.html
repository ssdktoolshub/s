📚 Clinical Pathology Notes: Lipid Ratios

Why are lipid ratios valuable?
Ratios offer a better indicator of cardiovascular plaque accumulation rates than single cholesterol values alone. They balance the concentration of plaque-forming lipoproteins (TC, LDL, TG) against protective clearance particles (HDL).

What are the three primary cardiac risk ratios?
1. **Total Cholesterol / HDL (Ideal < 3.5)**: Measures total atherogenic balance. Ratios > 5.0 suggest elevated plaque progression.
2. **LDL / HDL (Ideal < 2.0)**: Standard indicator of bad vs good cholesterol ratios. Ratios > 3.5 suggest higher risks.
3. **Triglyceride / HDL (Ideal < 2.0)**: A strong surrogate marker for insulin resistance, metabolic syndrome, and small dense LDL particles (atherogenic) if > 4.0.
