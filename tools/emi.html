Frequently Asked Questions

What is Equated Monthly Installment (EMI)?＋
An EMI is a fixed payment amount made by a borrower to a lender at a specified date each calendar month. EMIs are applied to both interest and principal each month so that over a specified number of years, the loan is paid off in full.
How does the principal and interest split change over time?＋
In the early stages of a loan, a large portion of each monthly payment goes toward paying off the interest because the outstanding principal balance is high. As the outstanding balance drops over time, the interest charge decreases, meaning more of your monthly EMI goes directly toward clearing the principal balance.
