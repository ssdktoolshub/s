📚 Clinical Pathology Notes: Aspartate Aminotransferase (AST)

What is AST/SGOT?
AST (Aspartate Aminotransferase), historically called SGOT, is an enzyme present in high concentrations in liver cells, heart muscles, skeletal muscles, kidneys, and red blood cells. Thus, elevations can reflect damage to tissues other than the liver.

How does it differ from ALT?
ALT is highly specific to the liver, whereas AST is found in multiple organs. Evaluating the ratio of AST to ALT is highly informative for identifying the source of tissue damage.
