📚 Clinical Pathology Notes: Free T4

What is Free T4 (fT4)?
Free Thyroxine (fT4) is the unbound, active form of thyroxine hormone produced by the thyroid gland. Unlike bound T4, Free T4 enters body tissues directly to fuel metabolic operations. It makes up only about 0.03% of total T4 in circulation.

Why test Free T4 alongside TSH?
TSH is the first-line screening tool, but Free T4 is essential to determine clinical thyroid conditions. For instance, high TSH with low fT4 confirms Overt Hypothyroidism, while high TSH with normal fT4 implies Subclinical Hypothyroidism.

What affects Free T4 measurements?
Pregnancy, estrogen-containing medications, severe systemic illness, or biotin supplements (which can cause analytical interference in lab assays) can influence measured thyroid levels. Always consult your clinic regarding biotin intake prior to testing.
