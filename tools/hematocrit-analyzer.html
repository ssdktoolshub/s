📚 Clinical Pathology Notes: Hematocrit (PCV)

What is Hematocrit (PCV)?
Hematocrit (also known as Packed Cell Volume or PCV) measures the percentage of your total blood volume that consists of red blood cells. It is a critical diagnostic index for monitoring hydration and red cell density.

What causes changes in PCV?
Low PCV usually aligns with anemias (either due to underproduction of cells or nutrient deficiencies) or fluid overload. High PCV indicates concentrated red cells, most commonly caused by dehydration or lung disorders.

How do values change with pregnancy?
In pregnancy, plasma volume increases significantly more than red blood cell volume. This causes a physiological dilution effect, lowering normal limits for Hematocrit (down to 33.0%).
