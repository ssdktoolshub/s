📚 Clinical Pathology Notes: Triglycerides

What are Triglycerides?
Triglycerides are the most common type of fat in your body. They store excess energy from your diet. Elevated triglycerides increase metabolic syndrome, fatty liver, and vascular disease risks.

What are the clinical classifications?
- **Normal (< 150 mg/dL)**: Lower cardiovascular plaque risk.
- **Borderline High (150 - 199 mg/dL)**: Early metabolic shifts.
- **High (200 - 499 mg/dL)**: Atherosclerosis risk.
- **Very High (≥ 500 mg/dL)**: Carries a critical risk of acute pancreatitis (inflammation of the pancreas), which is a medical emergency.
