📚 Clinical Pathology Notes: Understanding MCHC

What is MCHC?
Mean Corpuscular Hemoglobin Concentration (MCHC) measures the average concentration of hemoglobin inside a given volume of packed red blood cells. Unlike MCH, it accounts for the actual cell volume.

How is it calculated?
The formula is: MCHC (g/dL) = (Hemoglobin (g/dL) × 100) / Hematocrit (%). For example, if Hb is 14.0 g/dL and Hematocrit is 42%, MCHC is `(14 × 100) / 42 = 33.3 g/dL`.

What do anomalies signify?
- **Low MCHC (< 32 g/dL)**: Microcytic/hypochromic anemia where cells are not fully saturated with hemoglobin.
- **High MCHC (> 36 g/dL)**: Red cells are overly concentrated, usually indicating hereditary spherocytosis or laboratory testing artifacts.
