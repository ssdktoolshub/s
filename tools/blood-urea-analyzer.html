📚 Clinical Pathology Notes: Blood Urea

What is Blood Urea?
Urea is a nitrogen-containing waste product formed in the liver during the breakdown of dietary and cellular proteins (urea cycle). It is transported via blood and excreted by the kidneys.

How does Urea differ from BUN?
Urea measures the entire molecular concentration of urea, while BUN (Blood Urea Nitrogen) measures only the nitrogen component of the urea molecule. The ratio of Urea to BUN is always 2.14.
