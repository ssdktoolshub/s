📚 Clinical Notes: Serum Iron

What is Serum Iron?
Serum iron measures the amount of iron circulating bound to transferrin in the blood. It reflects only the transport pool, not total iron stores.

Why test serum iron?
Serum iron helps diagnose iron deficiency anemia, iron overload disorders like hemochromatosis, and anemia of chronic disease. It must always be interpreted alongside TIBC and ferritin.

When is the best time to test?
Morning fasting samples yield the most reliable serum iron results, as levels fluctuate significantly throughout the day and are highest in the morning.

🩺 Clinical Note: Serum iron alone is not sufficient; interpret with TIBC and ferritin for a complete iron study profile.
