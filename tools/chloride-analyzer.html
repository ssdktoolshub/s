📚 Clinical Pathology Notes: Chloride

What is Chloride's role?
Chloride is the major extracellular anion. It works with sodium to maintain proper hydration and osmotic balance. It also directly influences the body's acid-base pH status, inverse to bicarbonate.

What are Hypochloremia and Hyperchloremia?
- **Hypochloremia (< 96 mEq/L)**: Low chloride, commonly caused by prolonged vomiting (loss of HCl acid in stomach), sweating, or chronic respiratory acidosis.
- **Hyperchloremia (> 106 mEq/L)**: High chloride, often caused by kidney disorders, severe dehydration, or metabolic acidosis.
