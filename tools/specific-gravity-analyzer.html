📚 Clinical Pathology Notes: Specific Gravity

What is Specific Gravity (USG)?
Specific gravity measures the concentration of solutes (like minerals, salts, and proteins) in urine compared to pure water. It is a highly sensitive metric for evaluating hydration.

What do abnormal USG values mean?
- **Low USG (< 1.005)**: Very dilute urine. Caused by high water intake, use of diuretics, diabetes insipidus (inability to retain water due to low ADH hormone), or severe chronic kidney disease.
- **High USG (> 1.030)**: Highly concentrated urine. Caused by dehydration, fever, SIADH (excessive ADH hormone holding water), or molecules like glucose (diabetes) or protein spilling into urine.
