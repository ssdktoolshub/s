📚 Clinical Pathology Notes: Thyroid Panel

How do endocrinologists read a thyroid panel?
Endocrinologists look at TSH alongside Free T4 and Free T3 together. Under negative feedback loops, if T4/T3 drop, the pituitary responds by skyrocketing TSH. Conversely, if the thyroid is hyperactive, high T4/T3 suppresses TSH down near zero.

What is Central/Secondary Hypothyroidism?
This is a rare form of underactive thyroid caused by a pituitary gland or hypothalamus issue rather than thyroid tissue damage. TSH is low or inappropriately normal, and Free T4 is low.

What is T3 Thyrotoxicosis?
A specific clinical presentation of hyperthyroidism where TSH is low and Free T3 is elevated, but Free T4 remains in the normal range. It is common in early hyperthyroidism or toxic nodular goiter.
