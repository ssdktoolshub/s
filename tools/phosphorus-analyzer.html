📚 Clinical Pathology Notes: Phosphorus

What is Phosphorus's role in the body?
Phosphorus (phosphate) is essential for bone structure, cellular energy production (ATP), cell membranes, and genetic material (DNA/RNA). It is regulated inversely with calcium by PTH and vitamin D.

What are Hypophosphatemia and Hyperphosphatemia?
- **Hypophosphatemia (< 2.5 mg/dL)**: Low phosphorus, often caused by vitamin D deficiency, hyperparathyroidism, or starvation refeeding. It causes muscle weakness and respiratory strain.
- **Hyperphosphatemia (> 4.5 mg/dL)**: High phosphorus, almost always caused by advanced kidney failure (inability to excrete phosphate) or hypoparathyroidism. It leads to calcium-phosphate crystal deposition in tissues.
