📚 Clinical Pathology Notes: Random Blood Sugar

What is Random Blood Sugar?
Random Blood Sugar measures blood glucose at any time of the day, regardless of when you last ate. It is a quick check to assess extreme deviations in blood sugar levels.

What are the ADA threshold limits?
According to the American Diabetes Association (ADA):
- **RBS (Random)**: Normal < 140 mg/dL, Prediabetes 140-199 mg/dL, Diabetes ≥ 200 mg/dL (especially if symptoms like polyuria/polydipsia are present).

What is Hypoglycemia?
Hypoglycemia refers to blood sugar falling dangerously low (usually < 70 mg/dL or 3.9 mmol/L). It requires immediate action (consuming 15 grams of fast-acting glucose, like juice or candy) to avoid complications.
