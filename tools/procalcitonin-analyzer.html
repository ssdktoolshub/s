📚 Clinical Pathology Notes: Procalcitonin

What is Procalcitonin (PCT)?
Procalcitonin is the peptide precursor of the hormone calcitonin. In healthy states, it is only produced in the thyroid. However, in response to bacterial endotoxins and inflammatory cytokines, PCT is synthesized widely throughout the body, rising rapidly within 2-4 hours.

Why is PCT useful in sepsis and antibiotic stewardship?
PCT levels correlate closely with infection severity. It helps differentiate bacterial infections (which cause high PCT) from viral infections or non-infectious inflammation (which keep PCT low). It is widely used to determine when to start or stop antibiotic therapy.
