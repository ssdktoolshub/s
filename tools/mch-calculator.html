📚 Clinical Pathology Notes: Mean Corpuscular Hemoglobin (MCH)

What is MCH?
Mean Corpuscular Hemoglobin (MCH) measures the average weight of hemoglobin present inside a single red blood cell. It indicates how much oxygen-carrying protein is packed inside individual cells, determining cell color.

How is MCH calculated?
The formula is: MCH (pg) = (Hemoglobin (g/dL) × 10) / RBC count (Million/µL). For example, if Hemoglobin is 15.0 g/dL and RBC is 5.0 Million/µL, then MCH is `(15.0 × 10) / 5 = 30 pg`.

What do Hypochromic and Normochromic mean?
- **Hypochromic (low MCH)**: Cells contain less hemoglobin, looking pale under the microscope. Typifies iron deficiency and thalassemia.
- **Normochromic (normal MCH)**: Red blood cells have a normal red color with proper hemoglobin levels.
