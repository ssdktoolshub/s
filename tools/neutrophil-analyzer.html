📚 Clinical Pathology Notes: Neutrophils

What are Neutrophils?
Neutrophils are the most abundant type of granulocytes and the first line of defense against bacterial infection. They migrate to the site of infection and engulf bacteria via phagocytosis.

What is Neutropenia?
Neutropenia is a abnormally low absolute neutrophil count (ANC < 1,500 cells/µL). It severely compromises your ability to fight off bacterial infections. Severe neutropenia (ANC < 500 cells/µL) is a medical emergency.

What causes Neutrophilia?
Neutrophilia refers to elevated counts (relative > 70% or absolute > 8,000 cells/µL), commonly triggered by acute bacterial infections, tissue necrosis, burns, inflammatory conditions, or emotional/physical stress.
