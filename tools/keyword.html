Frequently Asked Questions

What is Keyword Density in SEO?＋
Keyword density is the percentage of times a keyword or phrase appears on a web page compared to the total number of words on that page. In SEO, it is used as a signal to determine if a web page is relevant to a specific search query.
What is an ideal Keyword Density score?＋
There is no single ideal score, but most SEO experts suggest maintaining a keyword density between **1% and 2.5%** for your target keyword. Going higher (e.g. above 4-5%) may be classified as "keyword stuffing", which search engines penalize.
What are stop words?＋
Stop words are commonly used words in a language (such as "the", "a", "an", "in", "to", "and", "or", "but") that search engine indexing algorithms are often programmed to ignore when analyzing textual database ranks, since they carry minimal standalone semantic meaning.
