📚 Clinical Pathology Notes: VLDL

What is VLDL Cholesterol?
Very-Low-Density Lipoprotein (VLDL) is synthesized by the liver to distribute triglycerides to body tissues. Because it contains high percentages of fats, elevated levels suggest high triglycerides and metabolic syndrome.

How is VLDL calculated?
Using standard lipid equations:
- In mg/dL: VLDL = Triglycerides / 5.
- In mmol/L: VLDL = Triglycerides / 2.2.
This is highly accurate only if Triglycerides are less than 400 mg/dL.
