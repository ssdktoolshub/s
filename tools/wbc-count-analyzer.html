📚 Clinical Pathology Notes: White Blood Cells (WBC)

What is a WBC Count?
A White Blood Cell (WBC) count measures the total number of leukocytes in your blood. White blood cells are the core units of your immune system, responsible for fighting off bacteria, viruses, allergens, and parasites.

Why check WBC levels?
WBC levels act as the alarm system of the body. An elevated count (leukocytosis) indicates that your body is fighting an active infection or inflammation. A low count (leukopenia) indicates that your body has weakened defenses and is prone to illness.

What are standard pediatric differences?
Infants and children have naturally higher WBC count reference ranges compared to adults (normal limit goes up to 19,000 cells/µL) due to developmental states and immune system training against environment pathogens.
