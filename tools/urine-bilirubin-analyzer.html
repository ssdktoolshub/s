📚 Clinical Notes: Urine Bilirubin & Jaundice

What is urine bilirubin?
Normally, urine should contain no detectable bilirubin. Only conjugated (direct/water-soluble) bilirubin can appear in urine, since unconjugated bilirubin is protein-bound and not filterable by the kidneys.

Why does bilirubin appear in urine?
When the liver is damaged (hepatitis, cirrhosis) or bile flow is blocked (cholestasis, gallstones, biliary obstruction), conjugated bilirubin builds up in the blood and overflows into urine — producing a characteristic dark, tea-colored appearance.

How is the dipstick test performed?
A dipstick reagent strip is dipped into fresh urine. The bilirubin pad changes color in proportion to bilirubin concentration. Results are graded from Negative to 3+ (Large). Trace and above are clinically significant and warrant follow-up serum testing.
