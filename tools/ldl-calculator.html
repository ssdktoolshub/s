📚 Clinical Pathology Notes: Calculated LDL

How is LDL calculated?
Low-Density Lipoprotein (LDL) 'bad' cholesterol is most commonly calculated using the **Friedewald Equation**:
LDL = Total Cholesterol - HDL - (Triglycerides / 5) (in mg/dL).

When is this equation inaccurate?
The Friedewald equation becomes highly inaccurate if Triglycerides are **≥ 400 mg/dL** (≥ 4.52 mmol/L), or if the patient is not fasting. In these scenarios, a direct enzymatic LDL measurement should be ordered.
