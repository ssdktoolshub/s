📚 Clinical Pathology Notes: Lipase

What is Serum Lipase?
Lipase is a water-soluble enzyme secreted by the pancreas that aids in the digestion of dietary lipids (fats). While trace amounts are found in other body fluids, the diagnostic concentration in blood is heavily pancreatic-specific.

Why is Lipase preferred over Amylase?
Lipase is highly specific to the pancreas and is not elevated in salivary diseases (unlike amylase). Moreover, lipase remains elevated in the bloodstream longer (often up to 7 to 10 days) after an acute pancreatitis attack, making it highly valuable for delayed evaluations.

What do high Lipase levels signify?
Elevations above 3 times the upper limit of normal (~300 to 450 U/L) are highly diagnostic of acute pancreatitis. Other causes of moderate elevation include pancreatic duct obstruction, severe kidney disease (due to reduced clearance), or pancreatic pseudocysts.
