📚 Clinical Pathology Notes: Rheumatoid Factor

What is Rheumatoid Factor (RF)?
Rheumatoid Factor consists of autoantibodies (proteins produced by the immune system) that mistakenly attack healthy tissues. They are directed against the Fc portion of IgG antibodies. RF is a primary screening marker for Rheumatoid Arthritis (RA).

Specificity of the RF Test
The RF test has relatively low specificity. Elevated levels do not guarantee a Rheumatoid Arthritis diagnosis, as RF can be present in healthy individuals (especially the elderly) or other conditions such as Lupus (SLE), Sjögren's syndrome, chronic hepatitis, and bacterial infections.

Benign Causes of Elevation and False Positives
Up to 15% of healthy individuals over the age of 65 may test positive for RF without symptoms of joint disease. Additionally, vaccinations, chronic infections (like Tuberculosis or Endocarditis), or transient viral illnesses can cause temporary, benign elevations in RF levels.
