📚 Clinical Pathology Notes: D-Dimer

What is D-Dimer?
D-Dimer is a protein fragment produced when a blood clot dissolves in the body. When a blood vessel is injured, the body creates clots (fibrin meshes) to stop bleeding. When the clot degrades, D-Dimer is released.

How is D-Dimer clinically useful?
D-Dimer has high negative predictive value. A normal D-Dimer level (<500 ng/mL FEU) essentially rules out dangerous deep vein thrombosis (DVT) or pulmonary embolism (PE) in patients with low-to-moderate pre-test clinical probability.
