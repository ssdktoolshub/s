📚 Clinical Pathology Notes: PT & INR

What are PT and INR?
Prothrombin Time (PT) measures how quickly blood clots via the extrinsic and common pathways. International Normalized Ratio (INR) is a standardized mathematical ratio calculated from PT to account for different laboratory reagents, ensuring consistent interpretation worldwide.

Why are therapeutic INR targets higher?
For patients taking oral anticoagulants like Warfarin to prevent strokes or blood clots, the target INR is intentionally elevated (commonly between 2.0 and 3.0) to thin the blood slightly, preventing clotting while managing bleeding risks.
