📚 Clinical Pathology Notes: Autoimmune Panels

Why combine RF, Anti-CCP, and ANA?
An autoimmune panel looks at multiple independent pathways of immune dysfunction. While RF and Anti-CCP target joint-specific autoimmune arthritis, ANA screens for broader antinuclear antibodies linked to systemic diseases like Lupus and Scleroderma. Combining them narrows the differential diagnosis.

Positive Predictive Value (PPV) in Rheumatoid Arthritis
While testing positive for RF alone has a modest predictive value, testing positive for both RF and Anti-CCP yields a positive predictive value of nearly 98-99% for Rheumatoid Arthritis, highlighting the strength of combined panel interpretation.

Benign Positives in Systemic Screens
Up to 25% of individuals can present with low-titer positive ANA or RF due to age, thyroiditis, active virus recovery, or chronic low-grade bacterial infections. Therefore, diagnosis must never rely solely on a positive lab panel but must align with active clinical symptoms.
