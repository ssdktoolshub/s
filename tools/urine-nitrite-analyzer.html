📚 Clinical Notes: Urine Nitrite Test

How does the nitrite test work?
Gram-negative bacteria (E. coli, Klebsiella, Proteus) convert dietary nitrates in urine to nitrites. The dipstick pad detects these nitrites via a colorimetric reaction, turning pink if positive.

What are causes of false-negative results?
Gram-positive bacteria (Staphylococcus saprophyticus, Enterococcus) do not produce nitrites and will not trigger a positive result. A short bladder dwell time (less than 4 hours) also reduces conversion, yielding false negatives despite active infection.

Combined interpretation with leukocyte esterase?
Nitrite positive + leukocyte esterase positive = high probability of bacterial UTI. Nitrite positive alone or leukocyte esterase positive alone carries moderate predictive value. Urine culture (midstream clean catch) remains the gold standard for definitive diagnosis.
