Frequently Asked Questions

What is Body Mass Index (BMI)?＋
Body Mass Index (BMI) is a simple estimate of body fat level based on an individual's height and weight. It is widely used by healthcare practitioners to quickly screen for potential weight-related risks.
Is BMI accurate for everyone?＋
No. BMI has limitations because it does not directly measure body fat percentage or body composition. For example, athletes with high muscle mass may be classified as "overweight" or "obese" despite having very low body fat. It is best used as a general indicator rather than a comprehensive diagnosis.
