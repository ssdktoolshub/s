📚 Clinical Pathology Notes: CRP & hsCRP

What is C-Reactive Protein (CRP)?
C-Reactive Protein (CRP) is an acute-phase reactant protein synthesized by the liver in response to inflammatory cytokines. Levels increase rapidly in response to infection, trauma, or tissue damage.

What is the difference between standard CRP and hsCRP?
Standard CRP evaluates severe acute inflammation (ranges 10 to >200 mg/L). hsCRP (High-Sensitivity CRP) measures minute trace levels of inflammation (below 10 mg/L) and is clinically used to gauge chronic vascular inflammation and predict future cardiovascular events.
