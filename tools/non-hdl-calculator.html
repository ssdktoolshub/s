📚 Clinical Pathology Notes: Non-HDL

What is Non-HDL Cholesterol?
Non-HDL Cholesterol represents the sum of all atherogenic lipoproteins that carry apolipoprotein B (apoB). This includes LDL, VLDL, and IDL. It is calculated by: `Non-HDL = Total Cholesterol - HDL`.

Why is it preferred over calculated LDL?
Non-HDL cholesterol is not affected by high triglyceride levels, does not require fasting, and captures all artery-blocking cholesterol particles. It is often a better predictor of cardiovascular disease risk.
