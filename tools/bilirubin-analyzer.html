📚 Clinical Pathology Notes: Bilirubin & Jaundice

What is Bilirubin?
Bilirubin is a yellowish pigment formed during the normal breakdown of red blood cells. It travels in blood to the liver, where it is conjugated (made water-soluble) and excreted in bile.

What is the difference between Direct and Indirect Bilirubin?
- **Indirect (Unconjugated) Bilirubin**: The form that travels in blood to the liver. High levels suggest hemolysis (red cell destruction) or liver processing issues.
- **Direct (Conjugated) Bilirubin**: The form processed by the liver to be excreted in bile. High levels suggest biliary tract blockages or hepatocellular injury.
