📚 Clinical Pathology Notes: Urine pH

What does Urine pH represent?
Urine pH reflects the renal excretion of hydrogen ions to maintain systemic acid-base equilibrium. A normal random sample typically ranges from 4.5 to 8.0.

How does pH correlate with Kidney Stones?
- **Persistent Acidic Urine (pH < 5.5)**: Promotes the crystallization of **Uric Acid** and **Calcium Oxalate** stones.
- **Persistent Alkaline Urine (pH > 7.5)**: Encourages the formation of **Calcium Phosphate** and **Struvite** (infection-related magnesium ammonium phosphate) stones.
