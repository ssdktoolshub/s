📚 Clinical Pathology Notes: Monocytes

What are Monocytes?
Monocytes are the largest mononuclear leukocytes in the circulation. Upon migrating into organs and tissues, they mature into macrophages (which engulf foreign invaders and debris) or dendritic cells (antigen-presenting cells).

What is Monocytosis?
Monocytosis is an elevated count (relative > 8% or absolute > 800 cells/µL). It indicates active phagocytic cleanup, typically triggered by chronic infections (Tuberculosis, Endocarditis), recovery from acute infections, or autoimmune flare-ups.

What causes Monocytopenia?
Monocytopenia is a sub-normal count (relative < 2% or absolute < 200 cells/µL). It is rare and typically points to severe bone marrow injuries, chemotherapy, glucocorticoid use, or blood infection states.
