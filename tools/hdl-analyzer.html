📚 Clinical Pathology Notes: HDL

What is HDL and why is it 'good'?
HDL (High-Density Lipoprotein) acts as a vascular scavenger. It carries excess cholesterol away from blood vessel walls back to the liver for excretion (reverse cholesterol transport), protecting against plaque accumulation.

What are the gender differences in reference ranges?
- **Men**: Low HDL is defined as < 40 mg/dL (< 1.04 mmol/L).
- **Women**: Low HDL is defined as < 50 mg/dL (< 1.30 mmol/L), due to hormonal protective factors.
- **Optimal protection**: For both genders, HDL ≥ 60 mg/dL (≥ 1.55 mmol/L) offers high cardiovascular protection.
