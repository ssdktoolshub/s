📚 Clinical Pathology Notes: De Ritis (AST/ALT) Ratio

What is the De Ritis Ratio?
Named after Fernando De Ritis, it is the ratio of serum Aspartate Aminotransferase (AST) to Alanine Aminotransferase (ALT). It is a valuable biomarker for classifying liver injuries.

How are the ratio values interpreted?
- **Ratio < 1.0**: Suggestive of NAFLD (fatty liver) or chronic viral hepatitis.
- **Ratio > 1.0 to 2.0**: Often indicates progression to liver cirrhosis.
- **Ratio ≥ 2.0**: Strong clinical indicator of alcoholic liver disease (since alcohol reduces ALT activity by decreasing vitamin B6 levels).
