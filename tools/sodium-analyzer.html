📚 Clinical Pathology Notes: Sodium

What is Sodium's role in the body?
Sodium is the primary extracellular electrolyte. It regulates blood volume, blood pressure, osmotic equilibrium, and nervous transmission.

What are Hyponatremia and Hypernatremia?
- **Hyponatremia (< 135 mEq/L)**: Low sodium, often caused by fluid retention, heart failure, kidney strain, or drinking too much water without electrolytes.
- **Hypernatremia (> 145 mEq/L)**: High sodium, almost always caused by severe water loss (dehydration) or excessive salt ingestion.
