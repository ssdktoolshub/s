📚 Clinical Pathology Notes: Alkaline Phosphatase

What is Alkaline Phosphatase (ALP)?
ALP is an enzyme present mostly in the liver (specifically in cells lining the bile ducts) and bones. Significant elevations are usually caused by biliary tract diseases or bone cell hyperactivity.

Why is children's ALP naturally higher?
Osteoblasts (bone-forming cells) release ALP as they lay down new bone tissue. During childhood and adolescent growth spurts, rapid bone growth naturally elevates ALP, which is normal and physiological.
