📚 Clinical Pathology Notes: Total Protein

What is Total Protein?
Total Protein measures the cumulative amount of protein in your blood, primarily representing Albumin and Globulins. It is a key indicator of nutritional status, liver synthetic capacity, and renal retention.

What does an abnormal Total Protein indicate?
- **Low levels**: Can suggest malabsorption, liver failure (inability to synthesize proteins), or kidney disease (nephrotic syndrome leaking proteins).
- **High levels**: Often caused by severe dehydration concentrating the blood, or chronic inflammatory diseases/multiple myeloma producing excessive antibodies (globulins).
