📚 Clinical Pathology Notes: Eosinophils

What are Eosinophils?
Eosinophils are a type of disease-fighting white blood cell. They are particularly active during allergic diseases (such as asthma, eczema, and drug allergies) and in defending the body against parasitic infections.

What is Eosinophilia?
Eosinophilia is an elevated eosinophil count (relative > 4% or absolute > 500 cells/µL). It indicates hyper-reactive allergic states, eosinophilic asthma, drug hypersensitivities, or worm infestations.

What is Eosinopenia?
Eosinopenia is an abnormally low eosinophil count. Because normal counts are already low, eosinopenia can be difficult to identify in routine lab tests. It is sometimes associated with steroid use or Cushing's syndrome.
