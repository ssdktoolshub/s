📚 Clinical Pathology Notes: Glucosuria

What is Glucosuria?
Glucosuria is the presence of glucose in urine. Normally, the kidneys reabsorb all filtered glucose. Spillage occurs when blood glucose levels exceed the **Renal Threshold** (approximately 180 mg/dL).

What are the primary causes?
- **Diabetes Mellitus**: The most common cause, where high blood sugars spill over the kidneys' filtration threshold.
- **Renal Glucosuria**: A benign condition where renal tubules fail to reabsorb glucose despite normal blood sugar levels.
- **Pregnancy**: Hormonal shifts can temporarily lower the renal threshold (Gestational Glucosuria).
