📚 Clinical Pathology Notes: Cortisol

What is Cortisol?
Cortisol is the primary glucocorticoid stress hormone synthesized by the adrenal glands. It plays vital roles in regulating glucose metabolism, suppressing inflammation, controlling blood pressure, and modulating the body's stress response.

Why does collection timing matter?
Cortisol follows a circadian rhythm (diurnal pattern). It peaks in the early morning (approx. 6:00 AM - 8:00 AM) to prepare the body for waking activities, and drops gradually throughout the day, reaching its lowest point (trough) around midnight. Evaluating timing is essential to avoid misinterpreting low or high levels.

What is Cushing's and Addison's Disease?
Adrenal insufficiency (Addison's disease) is characterized by pathologically low cortisol levels. Conversely, hypercortisolism (Cushing's syndrome) represents prolonged excessive cortisol production, often presenting with central obesity, muscle weakness, and hypertension.
