📚 Clinical Pathology Notes: Absolute Neutrophil Count

What is ANC?
Absolute Neutrophil Count (ANC) calculates the real number of neutrophils per microliter of blood. It is the primary clinical indicator used to assess a patient's risk of infection during chemotherapy or immunosuppression.

What are Segmented vs. Band Neutrophils?
Segmented neutrophils are fully mature cells, whereas band neutrophils are slightly immature cells. Both actively fight infection. The formula includes both: ANC = WBC × (%Seg + %Bands) / 100.

How is Neutropenia classified?
- **Normal**: ANC ≥ 1,500 cells/µL
- **Mild Neutropenia**: ANC 1,000 to 1,500 cells/µL (minimal infection risk)
- **Moderate Neutropenia**: ANC 500 to 1,000 cells/µL (moderate infection risk)
- **Severe Neutropenia**: ANC < 500 cells/µL (severe, life-threatening infection risk).
