📚 Clinical Notes: Urine Ketones

What are Ketones?
Ketones (acetoacetate, beta-hydroxybutyrate, acetone) are produced when the body burns fat for energy instead of glucose. They are excreted through urine and breath.

When is this dangerous?
In diabetic patients, significant ketonuria (Moderate to Large) combined with high blood glucose and acidic blood pH indicates **Diabetic Ketoacidosis (DKA)** — a life-threatening emergency requiring immediate hospital care.
