📚 Clinical Pathology Notes: Blood Urea Nitrogen (BUN)

What is BUN?
BUN stands for Blood Urea Nitrogen. It measures the amount of nitrogen in your blood that comes from the waste product urea. It is a key indicator used to assess renal clearance and metabolic protein loads.

What is the math behind Urea to BUN?
Urea has a molecular weight of 60.06 g/mol, and contains 2 nitrogen atoms (28.01 g/mol). Thus, BUN makes up 46.6% of the urea molecule. The formula is:
BUN (mg/dL) = Urea (mg/dL) / 2.14.
