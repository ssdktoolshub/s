📚 Clinical Pathology Notes: Insulin & HOMA-IR

What is Fasting Insulin?
Insulin is an anabolic hormone secreted by pancreatic beta cells. Fasting insulin levels measure the baseline amount of insulin circulating when blood sugar is low, which regulates gluconeogenesis. Normal fasting levels are between 2 and 25 μU/mL, though optimal functional ranges are often lower (under 10 μU/mL).

What is HOMA-IR?
The Homeostatic Model Assessment of Insulin Resistance (HOMA-IR) is a mathematical estimation of insulin sensitivity. It represents the balance between hepatic glucose output and insulin secretion. The formula is: HOMA-IR = (Fasting Insulin [μU/mL] × Fasting Glucose [mg/dL]) / 405.

What do the HOMA-IR ranges mean?
A score under 1.0 represents optimal insulin sensitivity. Scores between 1.0 and 1.9 indicate borderline or early insulin resistance. A HOMA-IR score of 2.0 or greater indicates significant insulin resistance, showing that the pancreas is overproducing insulin to clear blood sugar. This is a primary risk factor for prediabetes, metabolic syndrome, and Type 2 diabetes.
