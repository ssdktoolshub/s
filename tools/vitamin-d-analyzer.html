📚 Clinical Pathology Notes: Vitamin D

What does 25-hydroxyvitamin D measure?
Vitamin D is measured in labs as 25-hydroxyvitamin D [25(OH)D]. It is the best indicator of overall Vitamin D status since it reflects resources built up from sun exposure, diet, and supplements. Active Vitamin D (1,25-dihydroxyvitamin D) is not a good indicator of reserves.

What are the main roles of Vitamin D?
Vitamin D's main function is to promote calcium absorption in the gut, ensuring proper bone mineralization and growth. It is also critical for neuromuscular function, immune response modulation, and reduction of systemic inflammation.

How is Vitamin D toxicity defined?
Vitamin D toxicity (hypervitaminosis D) occurs at levels exceeding 100 ng/mL (often >150 ng/mL) and is almost exclusively caused by mega-doses of supplements, not sun exposure. It leads to hypercalcemia, causing kidney damage and calcium buildup in cardiovascular tissue.
