📚 Clinical Pathology Notes: Magnesium

What is Magnesium's role?
Magnesium acts as a cofactor for over 300 enzyme reactions, regulating muscle contractions, cardiac conduction, and bone mineral pathways.

What are Hypomagnesemia and Hypermagnesemia?
- **Hypomagnesemia (< 1.7 mg/dL)**: Low magnesium, often caused by malnutrition, alcoholism, or loop diuretics. It causes muscle hyper-excitability.
- **Hypermagnesemia (> 2.2 mg/dL)**: High magnesium, commonly caused by renal failure combined with ingestion of magnesium-containing antacids or laxatives. It causes muscle weakness and slow reflexes.
