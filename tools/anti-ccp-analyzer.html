📚 Clinical Pathology Notes: Anti-CCP

What are Anti-CCP Antibodies?
Anti-Cyclic Citrullinated Peptide (anti-CCP) antibodies are autoantibodies produced by the immune system that target citrullinated proteins. These proteins are created during cellular inflammation in the joints. Anti-CCP is a highly specific marker used to diagnose Rheumatoid Arthritis (RA).

Specificity vs. Rheumatoid Factor (RF)
While Rheumatoid Factor is sensitive, it has low specificity and can be positive in healthy elderly people or other chronic infections. In contrast, Anti-CCP boasts an outstanding specificity of 95-98%, making a positive result highly indicative of RA even in the early stages of the disease.

Joint Destruction Risk
The presence of strong positive Anti-CCP antibodies is associated with a significantly higher risk of progressive, erosive joint destruction and severe radiographic joint damage. It is a key prognostic marker rheumatologists use to guide aggressive early treatment.
