📚 Clinical Pathology Notes: Red Blood Cells (RBC)

What is an RBC Count?
An RBC count measures the number of red blood cells in a microliter of blood. Red blood cells are shaped like tiny donuts without holes and are packed with hemoglobin to transport oxygen to cells.

Why is the RBC Count crucial?
It checks your blood's structural health. If your RBC count is low, you have erythrocytopenia, meaning your body is struggling to distribute oxygen. If it is high, you have erythrocytosis, which may make the blood thick and sluggish.

How do values change with age and gender?
Men generally have higher RBC counts than women because testosterone stimulates red cell production. Children have different reference ranges, and pregnant women have lower limits due to blood volume expansion.
