📚 Clinical Pathology Notes: Lymphocytes

What are Lymphocytes?
Lymphocytes are mononuclear WBCs essential for adaptive immunity. They divide into T-cells (directly destroy infected cells), B-cells (produce custom antibodies), and Natural Killer (NK) cells.

What causes Lymphocytosis?
Lymphocytosis is an elevated count (relative > 40% or absolute > 4,800 cells/µL). It is typical during viral infections (mononucleosis, cold, hepatitis, chickenpox), chronic infections, or lymphoid leukemia (CLL).

What is Lymphopenia?
Lymphopenia refers to sub-normal counts (relative < 20% or absolute < 1,000 cells/µL). This leaves you vulnerable to viral pathogens and can indicate general immune suppression, HIV infection, or systemic corticosteroid treatment.
