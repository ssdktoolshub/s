📚 Clinical Pathology Notes: Cardiac Troponins

What is Cardiac Troponin?
Cardiac Troponins (cTnI and cTnT) are regulatory proteins controlling calcium-mediated interaction of actin and myosin. They are highly specific biomarkers for myocardial (heart muscle) injury.

Why are Troponin levels measured?
They are primarily measured to diagnose Acute Coronary Syndrome (ACS) and Acute Myocardial Infarction (heart attack). They also rise in other conditions causing cardiac strain, such as myocarditis, severe heart failure, or pulmonary embolism.
