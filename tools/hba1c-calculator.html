📚 Clinical Pathology Notes: HbA1c & ADAG Formula

What is HbA1c?
HbA1c (Glycated Hemoglobin) is formed when glucose in the blood sticks to hemoglobin in red blood cells. Since red cells live for about 3 months, HbA1c represents the weighted average sugar levels over 90-120 days.

What is the ADAG conversion formula?
The ADAG (A1c-Derived Average Glucose) study defined the mathematical relationship between HbA1c and Average Glucose (eAG):
HbA1c (%) = (eAG mg/dL + 46.7) / 28.7.

What do the percentages represent?
- **Normal**: Under 5.7%
- **Prediabetes**: 5.7% to 6.4% (high risk of developing diabetes)
- **Diabetes**: 6.5% or higher.
