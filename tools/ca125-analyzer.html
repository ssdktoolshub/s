📚 Clinical Pathology Notes: CA-125

What is CA-125?
Cancer Antigen 125 (CA-125) is a protein most heavily present on the surface of ovarian cancer cells. It is also found in smaller amounts in normal tissues like the lining of the abdomen, pelvis, and chest. It is clinically utilized as a tumor marker.

Can benign conditions raise CA-125?
Yes, absolutely. CA-125 is notoriously elevated in several non-cancerous conditions, especially in pre-menopausal women. These include menstruation, pregnancy, endometriosis, pelvic inflammatory disease (PID), uterine fibroids, and liver cirrhosis.
