📚 Clinical Pathology Notes: Understanding ESR

What is Erythrocyte Sedimentation Rate (ESR)?
ESR is a blood test measuring how quickly red blood cells (erythrocytes) settle at the bottom of a vertical test tube in one hour. When inflammation is present, blood proteins (like fibrinogen) clump RBCs together, making them heavier so they settle faster, resulting in an elevated ESR.

What does a high ESR level mean?
An elevated ESR points to active inflammation in the body. It does not identify the exact source of inflammation, but acts as a diagnostic indicator for infections, autoimmune diseases (rheumatoid arthritis, lupus), tissue damage, or certain malignancies.

How do reference ranges adapt with age?
ESR naturally increases with age. For older adults (over 50), the upper normal bound shifts upward (up to 20 mm/hr for men and 30 mm/hr for women). Some clinical formulas estimate this as `Age / 2` for men and `(Age + 10) / 2` for women.
