📚 Clinical Pathology Notes: Folate

What is Folate (Vitamin B9)?
Folate is a water-soluble B vitamin naturally present in green leafy vegetables, beans, and citrus fruits. Folic acid is the synthetic form used in fortified foods and supplements. It is essential for cell division and DNA synthesis.

Why is folate critical during pregnancy?
Folate is absolutely vital during early pregnancy for the proper development of the fetal brain, skull, and spinal cord. Adequate folate prevents up to 70% of neural tube defects (NTDs) like spina bifida. Women planning pregnancy are advised to take 400 mcg of folic acid daily.

How do Folate and Vitamin B12 interact?
Folate and B12 are co-factors in red blood cell production. Folate supplementation can resolve anemia symptoms caused by Vitamin B12 deficiency but does not fix B12-induced neurological damage, potentially masking a critical B12 deficiency. They should ideally be checked together.
