📚 Clinical Pathology Notes: NT-proBNP

What is NT-proBNP?
NT-proBNP is a non-active prohormone released by cells in the heart ventricles in response to stretching caused by increased pressure or fluid volume overload. It is an established biomarker for heart failure.

How does Age affect NT-proBNP cutoffs?
Kidney function naturally declines and subclinical cardiac changes occur with age. Therefore, higher thresholds are utilized to rule in heart failure in older patients to minimize false-positive classifications.
