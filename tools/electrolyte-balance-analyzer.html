📚 Clinical Pathology Notes: Anion Gap & Electrolytes

What is the Anion Gap?
The Anion Gap is a mathematical calculation representing unmeasured anions in the blood (like albumin, phosphates, and organic acids). The equation is:
Anion Gap = Sodium - (Chloride + Bicarbonate). It is primarily used to identify and classify causes of Metabolic Acidosis.

What does an elevated Anion Gap mean?
An elevated gap (> 16 mEq/L) suggests an accumulation of unmeasured metabolic acids in the blood. Common causes include Diabetic Ketoacidosis (DKA), Lactic Acidosis (from tissue hypoxia or sepsis), Uremia (kidney failure failing to excrete acids), or toxic ingestions.
